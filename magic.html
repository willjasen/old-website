Wow...where do I begin...

First of all, when you're doing magic for a business, you should never, ever do it for free, and that means just for tips.

The reason is that you're providing a service to the restaurant and they should pay for it.

On top of that, some of the waitstaff may think you're "stealing" their tips if you're working just for tips, that is, that you're getting the money that would have been left to them.

You have to remember that no restaurant needs someone to walk around and do magic tricks.

They do need someone to extend some hospitality and to entertain their guests while they're waiting for their food or check to arrive.

Also, a magician gives restaurant patrons another reason to go that restaurant as opposed to another restaurant.

You have to remember that a restaurant or any business for that matter is around to make money...not to provide a magician with an income. If you can help them raise their bottom line, then you'll have a steady job.

The thing I'd caution you about is to make sure you're ready for this type of thing. It's one thing to do magic when you want to do it, for your friends, etc. It's another thing to HAVE to do it because it's 5:00 PM on a Tuesday afternoon.

So to answer your question...never, ever, ever work for free.

Just as a ballpark figure, $50 for 3 hours at a restaurant is well on the low scale, but a good place to start. This is assuming you have at least a 6 week contract with the restaurant.

Well, I've rambled on enough. If you have any more questions, feel free to ask away!
